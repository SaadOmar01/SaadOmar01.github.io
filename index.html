General Information and Key Aspects:

The Region of North Africa during both Ancient and Medieval times is extremely rich in history with various powers both external and internal arising and forming Empires the major examples that marked their presence for centuries include,The Egyptian Kingdom,The Assyrian Empire, Achaemenid Persian Empire, Phoenicians, Carthaginians, Greeks, Numidians, Macedonian Empire, Ptolemaic Empire, Romans, Vandals and Germanic Northern European peoples as well as,Byzantine, Rashidun, Ummyad and Abbassid Caliphates, Almorvaid,Zirid and Almohad Caliphate Berber Empires, Fatimids, Ayyubids, Mamluks and finally the Ottomans. Other dynasties that controlled the region for decades up to a century preodminantly include Turkic origin dynasties such as, The Seljuk Zengrids, Tulunids and Ikhshidid dynasties. Each one of these Empires, dynasties, emirates and Kingdoms altered the dynamics of North Africa till the present day and will continue to do so into the future. The North African Region is situated near to various superpowers and other neighbourging continents and nations. To the North Southern Europe and the Mediterennean Sea. To the East Western Asia, levant and the Middle East. To the West the Iberian pennisula and the Atlantic Ocean and to the south African Sub-Saharan Countries.More detail and analysis will be covered throughout the article with an array of sources and evidences from across the international scene.

The area of North Africa encompasses two major areas. Egypt which is the gateway from Africa into Asia and the Countries to the west of Egypt primarily known as the Maghreb which encompasses countries such as, Libya, Tunisia, Algeria, Morocco and Mauritania. The North African region has always been dominated and inhabited by the proto-berber tribes before that the peoples who had inhabited the region where Afro-Asiatic, Iberomaurisian, neolithic also a significant sum of links to the north eurasian steppe which many claim could have been assimialited from the yamnaya culture. In later History as Nations and Empires began to develop mass migrations to north africa commenced and the Empires and Dynsaties mentioned prior began to settle and assimilate.
